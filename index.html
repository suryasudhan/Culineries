Cusines Aviailable

Andhra Cuisine

A complete vegetarian Andhra meal typically consists of rice served with ghee, pulihora, chapati or puri, pappu (lentils), sambar, chaaru (rasam), fried and wet curries, appadam (papadum), odiyalu, chutney, pachadi, avakaya, yoghurt and a sweet for dessert.

Tamilian Cuisine

Tamilian cuisine features rice, lentils, and spices like tamarind and coconut. Popular dishes include idli, dosa, sambar, rasam, and kuzhambu. Meals are often served on banana leaves with sides like appalam and desserts like payasam.

Kerala Cuisine

Kerala cuisine features rice, coconut, and spices like black pepper and cardamom. Popular dishes include fish curry, sambar, and avial, often served on banana leaves during a traditional sadhya feast. Influences from Malabar Muslim and Syrian Christian communities add to its rich diversity.

Bengali Cuisine

Bengali cuisine, from the Bengal region, emphasizes rice, fish, and a variety of spices like mustard oil. Popular dishes include hilsa fish curry, shukto (a mixed vegetable dish), and sweets like rasgulla and sandesh.

Gujarati Cuisine

Gujarati cuisine is predominantly vegetarian and features dishes like dhokla, thepla, and undhiyu. Meals often include a variety of farsan (snacks), dal, roti, rice, and shaak (vegetable dishes), typically served in a thali. The cuisine is known for its unique blend of sweet, salty, and spicy flavors

Rajasthani Cuisine

Rajasthani cuisine is known for its rich, spicy flavors and use of ghee. Signature dishes include dal baati churma, laal maas (spicy meat curry), and gatte ki sabzi (gram flour dumplings in yogurt curry). Meals often feature a variety of breads like bajra roti and desserts such as ghewar

Mughalai Cuisine

Mughlai cuisine, originating from the Mughal Empire, is known for its rich, aromatic dishes featuring spices like saffron, cardamom, and cloves. Popular dishes include biryani, kebabs, and creamy gravies like korma and butter chicken. The cuisine often incorporates nuts, dried fruits, and yogurt for a luxurious taste

Uttar Pradesh Cuisine

Uttar Pradesh cuisine features a mix of vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes, with staples like dal, roti, and rice. Popular dishes include aloo kachori, chole bhature, and Lucknowi biryani. The cuisine is known for its diverse flavors, influenced by Awadhi, Mughlai, and Bhojpuri traditions

Punjabi Cuisine

Punjabi cuisine is known for its rich, buttery flavors and includes both vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes. Popular items are sarson da saag (mustard greens stew) with makki di roti (cornmeal flatbread), and tandoori chicken. The cuisine features bold spices and generous use of ghee
